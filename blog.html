TRAVELING TO THE MOUNTAINS

Mountains GJALICA



Mountains Gjalica's vegetation is rare. On the north-west side grow: beech (which spreads over 1500 to 1800m above sea level) and pine. Eastern slopes rich alpine pastures. In the Gjalica Mountain, some of the endemic plants of the Kukes region are encountered: Albanian vylet, Albanian lily (Lilium albanicum Griseb), lari (the only coniferous tree that falls on the leaves), spruce, rosemary, white fir, poplar wild and so on. Gjalica's mountain is mainly covered with beech and pine forests, which are currently heavily damaged by a number of factors. During the communist regime, Malj Gjalica's forests were used for timber.

Geologically, the terrain of the surrounding area is dominated by mountainous and high terrain. The city sprawls across the Luma Plain within the Albanian Alps between the banks of Lake Fierza and the hills of the northernmost Korab Mountains and westernmost Shar Mountains.
